Script

Three inject types in one beat, in strict order: settle the body (B3), activate it (B5), then reposition it spatially (C1). "You know what to do now" signals this is the fourth repetition of the check-in structure; the phrase itself is a regulation tool.

Chapter 013 — full body listening and fake listening
Full body listening and fake listening 013_01 → 013_QIM
013_01_STU — shared audio, both players
013_01_STU → theory framing
Script
"A key part of empathy is listening. Sometimes we think we are listening but actually we are fooling ourselves and others all the time. It's easy to fake listen. To be listened to is a striking experience partly because it is so rare… Fake listening is where our awareness is primarily on ourselves and our self-talk: our own thoughts, feelings, judgments and conclusions. We can appear to be listening and even make encouraging noises — but our internal dialogue is quite different. When we seek to engage and empathise with a patient we need to move beyond our own thoughts and needs. Let's try some fake listening."
G23 shared-theory A2 professional identity framing REFLECT linear frames → discomforts
The closing line — "Let's try some fake listening" — functions as a miniature G16 scenario handover. The theory inject announces the pedagogical trap before it is set. This is deliberately transparent: participants know they are about to fake-listen, which makes the felt experience more analytically available in debrief.

Chapter 014 — real listening (the counter-definition)
Real listening 014_01_STU / 014_01_SUP
Shared audio, both players
014_01_STU → theory counter-inject
Script
"Real listening, also known as full body listening, is listening without our own thoughts or personal agenda intruding. We notice what the other person says and how they say it — also picking up on what is unsaid. It includes non-verbal cues: leaning forward and making eye contact, for example. In full body listening all of our awareness is intently on the other person: their words, tone of voice, pauses, facial expressions, posture, emotions, energy and rhythm. We notice what lights them up and what deflates them. Our focus is so fixed on them that our own mental chatter vanishes and we speak with spontaneity. We also notice how our responses impact the other person."
G23 shared-theory E6 full-body listening (definition) REFLECT linear frames, shifts perspective
This chapter exists entirely to install the counter-vocabulary while the fake-listening experience (Chapter 013) is still physically present in the body. By naming full-body listening now — before asking anyone to practise it — the script ensures participants have a target concept when they enter Chapter 016. This is catalogue composite pattern 10.12 (the diptych): the wrong version is experienced first so the right version has something to contrast against.

Structural summary — what the annotation reveals

Chapters 013 and 016 are structurally identical. The same slot sequence — simultaneous-paired → role swap → timed debrief — appears in both chapters. The only change is which inject fills each slot. E7 fake-listening becomes E6 full-body listening. D6 internal distraction becomes D3 pain recall. The QIM layer disappears. This is catalogue composite pattern 10.12 made legible: the wrong version is experienced first so the right version has something to land against.

The QIM is invisible without annotation. In the script it appears as a footnote section (013_QIM). The annotation reveals it as a separate audio layer running continuously under beats 013_03 and 013_04 — which is the entire point of the exercise. Without tagging it as D6 with layered, continuous timing, any generative system would treat it as optional flavour text.

The check-ins are cumulative, not repetitive. Chapters 012 and 015 each deploy B3 + B5. The fifth check-in opens with "It's been a lot" — an acknowledgement of load that the earlier check-ins do not carry. Repeated structure with escalating emotional weight is itself a pattern an AI needs to understand.

Inject count across five chapters: approximately 22 distinct inject instances, covering 15 catalogue types (B3, B5, C1, D3, D6, E6, E7, E8, E9, G6, G7, G8, G10, G22, G23, G24, G26), 4 timing patterns (linear, layered, timed, simultaneous-paired / staggered-paired), and all 6 mode tags (ACT, SPEAK, REFLECT, ATTEND, PRESSURE, PAUSE).
